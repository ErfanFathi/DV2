A bar chart is a type of chart or graph that presents categorical data using rectangular bars. The length of each bar represents the frequencies of the five scientific names, and these values can vary for each city.

A heatmap is a data visualization technique that represents the magnitude of a phenomenon through color in two dimensions. On the x-axis, we have the five cities of the state, and on the y-axis, we represent the five tree types.

A stacked bar chart is a type of bar chart that illustrates the total magnitude of a data series by stacking individual values on top of one another. It is used to represent the heights of five types of trees in five different cities.
